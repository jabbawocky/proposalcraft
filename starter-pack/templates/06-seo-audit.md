**What I can see without digging yet:**

[Choose what applies: "The site has solid domain authority but the on-page structure is working against it." / "There's a lot of content but it's not organised around how people actually search." / "You're ranking for your brand name but not for any commercial intent keywords — which means you're only visible to people who already know you."]

That's the gap the audit will map in full.

**The audit — 2 weeks:**

A proper technical and strategic audit across four areas:

1. **Technical:** Crawl errors, indexation issues, site speed (Core Web Vitals), mobile performance, internal linking structure
2. **On-page:** Title tag and meta description quality, heading structure, content gaps, cannibalisation issues
3. **Keywords:** What you're currently ranking for, where the quick-win opportunities are, what your competitors rank for that you don't
4. **Backlinks:** Profile quality, toxic links, gap analysis vs top 3 competitors

Deliverable: a prioritised audit report. Not a 200-page PDF that sits in a folder — a focused document with a ranked action list: quick wins (do these first, low effort high impact), medium-term (structural improvements), and long-term (content investment).

**The 90-day strategy (starts after audit):**

Month 1: Quick wins. Technical fixes, on-page optimisation of your top 20 existing pages, title/meta rewrites.

Month 2: Content. Build out 4–6 pieces targeting identified keyword opportunities. Properly researched, written to rank, not just written.

Month 3: Authority. Identify link-building opportunities — guest posts, digital PR, resource link reclamation.

At 90 days: a full performance review against baseline. Search impressions, click-through rate, keyword movement, and if there's any conversion tracking in place, organic revenue.
