I watched the reference videos you shared and looked at what you're trying to explain. I have a clear picture of what would work.

**The problem with most explainer videos:**

They're written from the inside. They explain what the product does, in the order it makes sense to the people who built it. The viewer — who doesn't know or care about the product yet — tunes out by the 15-second mark.

What works is a different structure: open with the problem (the thing your viewer is already feeling), show them a world where that problem is gone, then and only then explain what you do.

That's the approach I'd take.

A [60 / 90 / 120]-second video. Tight, watchable, does one job: makes the right person want to know more.
