[The current mark looks dated / The logo works in isolation but falls apart in context / There's no system — the colours and type choices are inconsistent across touchpoints]. For a business at your stage, that gap costs you credibility before a word is read.

**What I'd deliver:**

A brand identity system that scales — from your email footer to a billboard.

**Phase 1 — Strategy (Week 1)**
A brand positioning brief: who you're for, what you stand for, and what you need to communicate at a glance. We do this before I design a single pixel. It's the difference between a logo you like and a logo that works.

**Phase 2 — Identity design (Weeks 2–3)**
Two directions — I don't believe in presenting twelve half-baked options. Two strong, considered directions with full rationale. You pick a direction, we refine. One round of refinement is included.

Deliverables:
- Primary logo + variations (horizontal, stacked, icon-only)
- Colour palette (primary, secondary, neutrals) — Hex, RGB, CMYK, Pantone
- Typography system (display + body, with usage rules)
- Basic usage guidelines (what to do, what not to do)

**Phase 3 — Application (Week 4)**
Business card, email signature, letterhead. Enough to see the system working in the real world and to hand off to anyone producing materials later.

**Final deliverables:** Everything in a single organised Figma file + exported assets folder (PNG, SVG, PDF). Plus a one-page brand guidelines PDF you can email to printers, developers, anyone who needs to stay on-brand.
