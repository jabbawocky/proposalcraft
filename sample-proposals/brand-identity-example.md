# Proposal: Brand Identity — Vessel Coffee

Hi Jenna,

I loved the brief — the tension you're describing ("specialty coffee, working-class spirit") is exactly the kind of positioning that makes for strong visual identity. Here's how I'd approach it.

## What I heard

Vessel is opening its second location and the visual identity that worked for a single shop is starting to feel inconsistent. You need a full rebrand that works across the espresso bar, retail bags, a merchandise line, and a growing social presence. The brand should feel earned, not designed — like it came up from the neighborhood, not from an agency.

## What I'll deliver

**Discovery & Strategy (Week 1)**
- 1-hour founder interview + site visit (or virtual tour)
- Competitive audit of 8 comparable coffee brands
- Brand positioning brief: personality, tone, what it's not

**Visual Exploration (Weeks 2–3)**
- 3 distinct creative directions: moodboards + rationale
- Down-select to 1 direction at end of week 3 review

**Identity Design (Weeks 4–6)**
- Wordmark + logomark (primary, secondary, monogram)
- Color palette (primary + secondary + neutrals)
- Typography system (2 typefaces with usage rules)
- 2 revision rounds included

**Application Design (Weeks 7–8)**
- Coffee bag label (1 roast)
- Takeaway cup sleeve
- Instagram post template (3 layouts)
- Business card

**Brand Handbook (Week 9)**
- Usage guidelines for all assets
- Do/don't examples
- File delivery: vector files, web-ready PNG/JPG, print-ready PDFs

**Not included:** Signage, environmental design, website design, merchandise beyond spec'd items.
